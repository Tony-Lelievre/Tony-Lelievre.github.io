## Modelling of instabilities in aluminium electrolysis cells

with <a href="http://www.inria.fr/fr/jean-frederic-gerbeau/">Jean-Fr&eacute;d&eacute;ric Gerbeau</a>, 
<a href="http://cermics.enpc.fr/~lebris">Claude Le Bris</a>
and Antonin Orriols.

#### <a href="../Pechiney"><img src="../assets/images-tony/francaistr.gif" nosave="" height="30" width="40"></a><a href="../Pechiney">Version fran&ccedil;aise</a>

The physical phenomena governing the behavior of aluminium
electrolysis cells are complicated and couple fluid mechanics, magnetic,
chemical reactions...  In our work, we focus on the magnetohydrodynamic
modelling of cells.

<blockquote><img src="../assets/images-tony/alu_cell.jpg" nosave="" height="200"></blockquote>

An electrolysis cells is composed of some anods, which drive a very intense
electric current through a bath (cryolite) and aluminium, both molten, towards
the cathod. We are especially interested in the modelling of the movements of
the interface between the two liquids (the bath and the aluminium).  These
movements are created by huge magnetic fields, due to the electric conductors
bringing the current to the cells.  <p>For the mathematical and numerical
questions raised by the modelling of this phenomenon, we refer to :
